Speech Central is a private speech pathology practice offering assessment and treatment services in a friendly but focused environment. Speech Central’s core area of practice is in helping clients to improve their speech clarity.

Contact us

Services

Communication is fundamental to a child’s development. Some children know what they want to say, but their speech is unclear and difficult to understand. This can be frustrating! It can also make forming relationships, learning, play and social interaction challenging.

In adulthood, unclear speech can continue to impact relationships and social interaction, as well as influencing career progression. For some people, being understood by others is a daily struggle.

Speech Central can help!

Speech Central can assist those with speech clarity issues through therapy for:

  • Articulation disorders
  • Phonological disorders
  • Motor speech disorders
  • Resonance disorders
  • Speech clarity issues related to palatal difficulties / anomalies
  • Accent modification

Evidence based therapy is used to help clients reach their full potential.

About

Cara Crage

Certified Practising Speech Pathologist

Cara is the owner and operator of Speech Central. She is a member of Speech Pathology Australia, registered as a Certified Practising Speech Pathologist and is Hanen trained (It Takes Two to Talk and Target Word). Cara received her Bachelor of Science from Curtin University, where she was awarded a position on the prestigious Vice Chancellor’s List for being in the Top 1% of all students at Curtin in her final year of study. She was also awarded three Board of Examiners letters of commendation for outstanding academic standards and was nominated for the Most Outstanding Clinical Performance Award.

During her career, Cara has worked on multi-disciplinary teams helping children with a range of medical conditions and developmental difficulties. She has gained experience in working with clients with a variety of complex communication disorders including treating children and young adults with various disabilities, acquired brain injuries and cleft lip and palate. Cara is also experienced in working with children who present with developmental communication difficulties. Cara currently splits her time between working at Princess Margaret Hospital and Speech Central. Her keen interest is in treating speech sound disorders.
